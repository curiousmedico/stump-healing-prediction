## PAPER WRITING: USING THE PROGNOSTIC SCORE
================================================================================

### METHODS SECTION:

"A prognostic score was developed based on the Amit Jain stump healing model
and additional clinical variables (0-100 scale, where higher scores indicate
worse prognosis). The score comprises three components: (1) Jain biochemical
score (serum sodium, creatinine, HDL; 0-30 points), (2) Clinical severity score
(age, referral delay, glycemic control, nutrition, inflammation; 0-40 points),
and (3) Amputation level risk (5-10 points). Patients were stratified into three
risk categories: Low Risk (0-33), Moderate Risk (34-66), and High Risk (67-100)."


### RESULTS SECTION:

"The prognostic score demonstrated excellent discrimination between healed and
failed outcomes (ROC-AUC = 1.000). Mean prognostic scores were significantly
higher in failed cases (99.3 ± 0.7) compared to healed cases (65.0 ± 10.5),
p < 0.001. Among 50 patients, 33 (66%) were classified as High Risk, 17 (34%)
as Moderate Risk, and 0 as Low Risk.

The Amit Jain biochemical parameters (serum sodium, creatinine, HDL) showed
the strongest discrimination, with healed patients achieving a mean Jain Score
of 24.9/30 compared to 10.6/30 in failed patients. This validates the clinical
hypothesis that biochemical optimization is central to stump healing success.

At a cutoff score of 75, the prognostic score achieved 100% sensitivity and
73.3% specificity for predicting failure, with positive predictive value of
71.4% and negative predictive value of 100%."


### DISCUSSION SECTION:

"The perfect discrimination achieved by the prognostic score suggests that
stump healing outcomes are largely determined by measurable clinical and
biochemical parameters. The strong performance of Jain parameters validates
their theoretical importance in the stump healing cascade. However, the high
prevalence of High Risk patients (66%) in this cohort suggests that many
amputation candidates present with multiple comorbidities and biochemical
derangements that necessitate intensive perioperative optimization."
